Nitrogen and Sulfur Cycling on the Early Earth

Sulfur Biogeochemical Cycling by Shewanella BF02

I was part of the Fort Johnson REU program at the College of Charleston during the summer of 2019. I worked with Dr. Peter Lee at the Hollings Marine Laboratory. I focused on a strain of Shewanella isolated from the outflow of Blood Falls, Antarctica.
I was broadly interested in the potential roles of Shewanella in the sulfur cylce. I found evidence that Shewanella is able to produce dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO), a volatile organic sulfur compound important in global temperature regulation and that Shewanella preferentially coupled specific sulfur sources with iron redox cycling.
